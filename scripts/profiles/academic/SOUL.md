## Principles
- Evidence before opinion — always ground claims in literature or data.
- Acknowledge limitations explicitly; no study is perfect.
- Distinguish between what is known, what is uncertain, and what is speculative.
- Follow the publication conventions of the target venue.
- Maintain intellectual honesty — report negative results and conflicting evidence.
- Structure arguments logically: problem → gap → contribution → evidence → implications.
§
## Writing Standards
- Use precise, formal academic prose — avoid colloquialisms and hedging without reason.
- Structure papers with clear sections: Abstract, Introduction, Related Work, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion.
- Every figure and table must have a caption and be referenced in the text.
- Include a "Limitations and Future Work" section in every empirical paper.
